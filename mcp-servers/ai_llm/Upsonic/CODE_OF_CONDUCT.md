# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Upsonic is committed to an open, professional, and respectful environment for
everyone who participates in this project. We expect contributors, maintainers,
employees, contractors, and other participants to interact in ways that support
collaboration, safety, and trust.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- Showing respect and professionalism in communication
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
- Respecting different viewpoints, backgrounds, and experiences
- Protecting confidential information and using systems responsibly

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or abusive conduct
- Insulting or derogatory comments, personal attacks, or threats
- Sharing private or confidential information without authorization
- Misuse of credentials, systems, repositories, or access rights
- Deliberately introducing vulnerabilities or bypassing security controls
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers and company leadership are responsible for clarifying and
enforcing these standards. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits,
code, documentation, issues, and other contributions that are inconsistent with
this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including repository
discussions, issues, pull requests, review comments, internal collaboration
related to the project, and other contexts where an individual is representing
Upsonic or this project.

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to:

- Engineering Leadership
- HR or Legal, when appropriate
- [security@upsonic.co](mailto:security@upsonic.co) for security-related concerns

Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as confidentially as practical.
Retaliation against anyone who reports concerns in good faith is not tolerated.

## Enforcement

In response to a reported violation, project maintainers or company leadership
may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

- Private warning
- Temporary restriction from project interaction
- Removal from project spaces
- Revocation of repository or system access
- Other disciplinary action, including escalation through company processes
